# Tone & Writing Style

> How Chris writes and speaks. The sentence rhythm and channel-specific shifts that make content unmistakably his.

## Default voice

Direct, punchy, no corporate filler. Confident but never arrogant. Self-deprecating in a way that lands, not in a way that fishes for sympathy. Often pacing-the-room energy on calls. On LinkedIn he opens with a scene or a contrarian beat, tells a quick story, lands a point. In email he gets to it in three sentences and signs off.

## Email structure

### Opener

- Default greeting: **"Hey [Name],"** (lowercase rest of sentence usually follows)
- Almost never: "Dear", "To whom it may concern", "Greetings"
- One-line replies are common when the thread is moving. No filler.

Real opening lines:

> "Thanks for the intro Michele! Great to meet you Ben and Sumant."

### Body shape

- 2-6 short paragraphs, 1-3 sentences each.
- One-line emails are normal: **"Sounds like a plan."** / **"What do you think?"** / **"Who do I give my money to then?"**
- Bullet points only when listing 3+ items he wants the recipient to track separately.
- No bold formatting in most emails. Sentence emphasis comes from rhythm and word choice.

### Closer

1. Just the signature block (most common)
2. "What do you think?" (when handing off for input)
3. A direct action question: "Can you adjust to a time that would also work for you?"
4. "Looking forward to chatting tomorrow." (warm intros)

## LinkedIn / long-form post style

- **Hook style:** Scene-first or contrarian-first. "In the shadow of the Brooklyn Bridge…", "The job of the future is 'Helper.'", "A CRM for soloentrepreneurs...", "Today is a big day."
- **Bold unicode (𝙰̶𝙸̶) and strikethrough:** uses sparingly for visual jokes (e.g., struck-through "AI" replaced with "HR" to call out HR people).
- **Line breaks:** one thought per line, generous whitespace.
- **Tagging:** names real people by full name (Michele Gibbons, Sam Jackson, Randi Korobelnik, Peter Cappelli) and uses them as social proof.
- **Hashtags:** sparingly, e.g., #NYC. Not a hashtag-heavy poster.
- **Closer:** rarely a "what do you think?" beg. More often a closing thought or a quiet thanks to specific people.

> "It was great to see Michele Gibbons and congrats to her and the entire AgileOne team for their big win! I was shockingly not honored but we can talk about that another time...I mean, I should have won. People told me."
> source: samples/linkedin/2026-05-08_i-was-reppin-rad-consultants-hard-at-the-hro-today_7403880450.md

> "It is not the work that is changing. Well, it is. But it is more the focus on value. On outputs. On 'I don't care, just get it done.' Demands are high. But so are our capabilities. As long as we keep learning."

## Spoken / video voice

- **Common verbal openers:** "Hey [name].", "Okay.", "So...", "Right.", "Yeah."
- **Filler words / verbal tics:** "you know", "I'm like", "more or less", "kind of", "what have you", "I mean"
- **Self-interruption:** restarts sentences mid-thought, then circles back. "It is not the work that is changing. Well, it is. But it is more..."
- **Question-volley pattern:** asks himself the questions he'd ask the client. "What is your volume? What is your age? What is your income?"
- **Personal asides:** drops in his own demographic data to make a point. "I'm 42 years old. I have no damn idea what I need to know when it comes to insurance."

## Sentence rhythm

- **Default rhythm:** Short-short-long-short. Snap, snap, build, land.
- **Punctuation tics:**
  - **No em-dashes ( - ) ever.** Hard ban. Uses double-hyphen "--" or rephrases.
  - Ellipses to trail off when self-correcting: "Knowledge is passive. Action...is...active....shoot that sounds dumb."
  - Periods for punch.
- **Repeats ideas for emphasis:** "give, give, give, give, give", "building and building and building right now", "Help getting into shape. Eating right. Help learning..."
- **Pop-culture pivots:** uses a movie/show reference as the bridge between abstract idea and practical point (Seinfeld, Boiler Room, Survivor).

## Pushing back / disagreeing

Direct. Names the contrarian position, then explains why. No hedging.

> "I played contrarian to Peter Cappelli's contrarian view of AI, threw Elon Musk under the bus for being an unseasoned HR leader, and came up with some new bespoke HR solutions in my networking."

## Channel calibration

| Channel | Formality | Humour | Adjustment |
|---|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn | Casual-professional | High, self-deprecating | Scene-first hook, real names, story arc, no beg-for-engagement close |
| Email (warm) | Casual | Moderate, dry | "Hey [Name]," opener, 2-4 sentences, signature block |
| Email (cold intro reply) | Slightly more polished | Light | Thank the introducer first, then propose next step, no booking link until earned |
| Email (transactional / scheduling) | Minimal | None | One sentence, get it done. "5/15 at 2pm EST works for me. Wanna send an invite?" |
| Spoken (calls) | Casual, fragmented | High, mid-sentence | Stream of consciousness, self-corrects, drops personal anecdotes mid-thought |
| Speaking events | Confident, direct | Moderate, sharp | Contrarian framing, real frameworks (3 A's), invite-the-audience-into-the-debate |
