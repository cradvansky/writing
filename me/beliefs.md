# Beliefs & Positions

> Hot takes, strong opinions, and the hills Chris will die on. Better to make these explicit than to let the AI invent flat versions.

## Core beliefs (non-negotiable)

### 1. AI is the path for solo operators to compete with teams of 10

The whole pitch. He's not selling labor reduction or "fewer people" - he's selling capability multiplication for the operators who are still doing every job themselves.

> "I am building this plane as I am flying it but if you want to have a look, let me know."
> source: samples/linkedin/2026-04-28_a-crm-for-soloentrepreneurs-i-tell-people-that-i-a_3290815488.md

> "I know what is possible and how low the barrier is to entry for hungry entrepreneurs. So how about this... Learn AI. Keep up with AI. Build a business that 'serves the slivers.' Price it low. Distribute it wide. Have AI iterate and improve and run the whole thing while delivering value."
> source: samples/linkedin/2026-04-27_the-job-of-the-future-is-helper-who-out-there-coul_1210656768.md

### 2. Give before you ask

"I'm all about give, give, give, give, give and then ask." Earn permission. Demonstrate value first. The booking link doesn't go in Email 1.

> "I'm all about give, give, give, give, give and then ask. So if all we're doing is we're being swell people that are just informing our constituents, our prospects, what have you. And we're asking for nothing. That's fine, you know, and then there comes a point though eventually where we do ask. You know, you put a small button in there, you put a form at the bottom of the page, whatever it might be."
> source: samples/granola/2026-05-27_catch-up_IT2KXd2r.md

### 3. Knowledge is passive. Action is active.

Reading sales books is not the same as moving the deal forward. Course-purchases are not the same as building the thing.

> "I read. This is not the first time you have read about me talking about sales books. But now it is a matter of 'what can those do for me and my business?' ... They are great for knowledge and even applying that knowledge to your sales operations. But are they actually doing something? No. Knowledge is passive. Action...is...active....shoot that sounds dumb. But let's get active! Hence, the CRM."
> source: samples/linkedin/2026-04-28_a-crm-for-soloentrepreneurs-i-tell-people-that-i-a_3290815488.md

### 4. The future of work is about value and outputs, not hours

The "I don't care, just get it done" school of work. Demands are high but capabilities have caught up.

> "It is not the work that is changing. Well, it is. But it is more the focus on value. On outputs. On 'I don't care, just get it done.' Demands are high. But so are our capabilities. As long as we keep learning."
> source: samples/linkedin/2026-04-21_as-expected-a-great-day-of-collaboration-networkin_7475826688.md

### 5. The job of the future is "Helper"

People with AI-built businesses freeing them up to do real human work in their communities. Income from the AI business funds the helping. He's not anti-AI doomer, not pro-AI utopian - pro-AI-as-a-means-to-good-work.

> "The job of the future is 'Helper.' Who out there couldn't use a little more help? Help getting into shape. Eating right. Help learning about something they are passionate with. Help cleaning up the neighborhood and city. Help with that elder that is a bit too far from the rest of their family ... we ... just need a little more help."
> source: samples/linkedin/2026-04-27_the-job-of-the-future-is-helper-who-out-there-coul_1210656768.md

> "Then become a helper. Do the things that need to be done that you really don't mind doing. That you take pride in accomplishing. Make this whole damn world a better place."
> source: samples/linkedin/2026-04-27_the-job-of-the-future-is-helper-who-out-there-coul_1210656768.md

### 6. Bespoke beats SaaS for the right buyer

He's not building generic SaaS. He's building tailored, integrated, modifiable applications for individual clients. The "modifiable on a quick message" is the selling point.

> "They are built bespoke for my clients, integrating with their systems and modifiable with a quick email / message to their friendly AI consultant - me!"
> source: samples/linkedin/2026-04-22_today-is-a-big-day-yes-it-is-wednesday-yes-it-is-e_3943857152.md

### 7. The 3 A's: Accountability, Awareness, Adaptability

His standard speaking framework for navigating the AI shift.

> "Humbled to be invited to speak. I brought to the forefront the need for accountability, awareness, and adaptability in this rapidly changing world of value."

## Hot takes / contrarian positions

### "We can do this ourselves" is almost always wrong (and a hidden compliment)

When a buyer says they'll build it internally, that's the strongest signal they see the value. He uses the Seinfeld Jerry/Kramer "levels" bit to land this.

> "A rebuttal that many folks in tech sales, such as myself, will get is that 'we can do this ourselves.' I love the ambition. The question then internally of course becomes 'Is it done right now? If not, when could it be done? Who will own that project? How many resources will you need internally to get it done?' ... 'We can do this ourselves' is probably the last thought that anybody in the company has about the solution that is actually valuable."
> source: samples/linkedin/2026-05-14_a-rebuttal-that-many-folks-in-tech-sales-such-as-m_6825883649.md

### Elon Musk is not an HR leader, and Peter Cappelli's contrarian-on-AI view is itself wrong

Won't dodge naming names when it serves the point.

> "I played contrarian to Peter Cappelli's contrarian view of AI, threw Elon Musk under the bus for being an unseasoned HR leader, and came up with some new bespoke HR solutions in my networking."
> source: samples/linkedin/2026-05-08_i-was-reppin-rad-consultants-hard-at-the-hro-today_7403880450.md

### Don't ask for the moon on the first date

Sales/marketing 101 - but constantly violated. He talks about pacing data capture, easing into asks via sliders and ranges instead of demanding exact numbers up front.

> "I remember I was hit up by a local insurance guy once and we had a nice conversation and he was telling me the whole time more or less in a polite way. And then he asked for, you know, me to open up every single financial book that I have. I'm like, I'm not doing that. I don't feel like digging. So he asked for the moon and the stars on the first date. And I'm like, nah."
> source: samples/granola/2026-05-27_catch-up_IT2KXd2r.md

### Self-built and modifiable beats generic SaaS for the right client

See belief #6 above. Pairs with the "we can do this ourselves" rebuttal.

## What Chris does NOT believe (avoid putting these in his mouth)

- Does NOT believe AI is replacing people. Frame is **buy back time for lean teams**, never **fewer people**.
- Does NOT believe big firms are bad. Many of his clients ARE large enterprises and he respects them.
- Does NOT do doomer takes about AI risk. He's a builder, not a pundit.
- Does NOT do hype takes about AI singularity or AGI timelines. He's grounded in real work.
- Does NOT moralize. Self-deprecating yes, preachy no.
