## 2. The Jerry & Kramer "levels" bit (Seinfeld)

**Use when:** Pushing back on "we can do this ourselves." The bit substitutes "custom AI web applications" for Kramer's "levels."

> "There are questions that generally, in my guess, don't get asked and answered after someone sees an external tool of value. 'We can do this ourselves' is probably the last thought that anybody in the company has about the solution that is actually valuable. So while you mull that over, let's look how Jerry and Kramer dealt with this. Instead of 'levels,' substitute 'custom AI web applications.'"

## 3. "Asking for the moon and the stars on the first date"

**Use when:** Talking about premature asks. Forms that demand full financial disclosure up front. Onboarding flows that scare prospects off.

## 4. "Give, give, give, give, give and then ask"

**Use when:** Sales sequencing, content marketing, earning trust. Five gives, then one ask - not a 50/50 split.

> "I'm all about give, give, give, give, give and then ask. So if all we're doing is we're being swell people that are just informing our constituents, our prospects, what have you. And we're asking for nothing. That's fine, you know, and then there comes a point though eventually where we do ask."

## What NOT to reach for

- Generic AI metaphors (the AI tide, the AI wave, the AI revolution) - too vague
- "Game changer", "paradigm shift", "next level" - banned (see vocabulary.md)
- "AI is like electricity / oil / fire" - overused, not Chris's voice
- Sci-fi references (Skynet, Terminator, HAL) - not his frame
- Sports analogies in general - he doesn't reach for these much; pop-culture (Seinfeld, Survivor, Boiler Room) is his lane
